# Skill: Narrative Storytelling

## Objective
Guide the user to structure their presentation around a compelling narrative by contrasting a negative "before" story with a positive "after" story. This makes technical points memorable and emotionally resonant.

## Core Principles (from `toastmasters-for-tech.md`)
This skill is an implementation of "The Narrative Arc (Advanced Storytelling)". The key is to move beyond abstract points and anchor them in concrete stories.

## Execution Steps
When a user wants to make their presentation more engaging or memorable, guide them through the following:

1.  **Identify the Core Message:** First, confirm the user's 1-3 key takeaways or critical factors. The stories must support these points.

2.  **Craft the "What Is" Story (The Problem):**
    *   Ask the user to describe the current, problematic state.
    *   Prompt them to create a story (real or illustrative) about a negative consequence of this problem.
    *   **Example Prompt:** "Let's tell a story about the 'before' state. Can you think of a time, even a hypothetical one, where not having [your solution] led to a bad outcome? For instance, a story about a company that ignored security warnings and what happened next. This creates the tension."

3.  **Craft the "What Could Be" Story (The Solution):**
    *   Ask the user to describe the ideal future state achieved with their solution.
    *   Prompt them to create a contrasting story that shows the positive benefits.
    *   **Example Prompt:** "Now, let's paint the 'after' picture. Tell a story about how your approach would have changed that negative outcome, or how it helped another company succeed. You want the audience to see the hero's journey, where your idea is the guide."

4.  **Connect to Key Factors:**
    *   Instruct the user to explicitly link their 1-3 key factors to the stories.
    *   **Example Prompt:** "Your stories are powerful. Now, let's connect them directly to your main points. You can say something like, 'This whole situation could have been avoided by focusing on [Critical Factor 1], which is why...' The story becomes the proof for your point."

5.  **Reinforce the Goal:**
    *   Remind the user that the audience will remember the stories long after they've forgotten the data points. The goal is for them to leave the room talking about the stories you told.
